## Feedback Track Summary

The Feedback track rewards people who help builders improve through live, recorded interaction.

It does not reward a builder simply for asking for feedback or receiving feedback.

Feedback track allocation:

- {{feedback_prize_pool}} {{reward_currency}}
- distributed proportionally by valid feedback credits

## Who Can Participate

People may participate in the Feedback track even if they are not builders.

Builders are also encouraged to participate.

A builder may earn Feedback track credits only when acting as the feedback giver for someone else's project. Builders giving feedback to other builders' projects follow the same rules as any other feedback giver — they must complete a qualifying live, recorded session through the official Discord flow.

Builders and feedback givers should join the {{community_name}} Discord and complete server verification before using the official event channels.

## Requirements

### 1. Use the Official Discord Flow

Qualifying feedback should be coordinated through the official feedback forum in the {{community_name}} Discord.

Flow:

- a builder posts a feedback request in the forum
- a feedback giver volunteers in that thread
- the builder team may accept or refuse a volunteer before the recorded session starts

This means the builder team chooses whether to proceed with a specific volunteer for that session.

Use the general discussion channel for broader questions and discussion.

Official links:

- {{community_name}} Discord: {{discord_url}}
- Feedback forum: {{discord_feedback_channel_url}}
- Discussion channel: {{discord_discussion_channel_url}}

### 2. Feedback Must Happen Live

Feedback must be given through a live interaction, such as:

- {{feedback_platform_recommendation}}
- Google Meet
- another comparable live meeting tool

### 3. Feedback Must Be Recorded

Each qualifying interaction must be recorded.

{{feedback_platform_recommendation}} is a recommended recording option. A project owner who requests feedback may create the meeting room, record the live session, and share the recording with the feedback giver for evidence.

Any comparable service is also acceptable if it creates a shareable recording that can be verified.

### 4. One Session Equals One Credit

Each qualifying feedback interaction counts as one credit.

The credit goes to the feedback giver for completing the live, recorded session through the official flow.

Receiving feedback does not create a credit by itself.

Offering feedback without completing the session does not count as a credit.

### 5. Maximum Credits

Each participant may earn up to {{max_feedback_credits_per_participant}} credits.

### 6. Evidence Must Be Shareable

Feedback evidence must be available for verification.

Participants should be prepared to share:

- the original feedback request post
- the volunteer coordination in the thread
- a link to the recorded session

## Payout Logic

The {{feedback_prize_pool}} {{reward_currency}} pool is divided proportionally across all valid credits earned by qualified participants.

In practical terms:

- builders do not earn Feedback track credits just for receiving feedback
- feedback givers earn credits for completed qualifying sessions
- builders may also earn credits if they give qualifying feedback to other teams

## Practical Standard

The goal of this track is to reward helpful, visible, real interaction that improves builder projects.
