# Design Principles

These are the evergreen ideas behind the Piece of Pie format.

They do not change between events. They are the reason this framework works.

---

## 1. Participation Over Competition

Traditional hackathons reward the best project.

This format rewards **consistent, verifiable participation**.

There are no judges ranking entries. Qualification is based on meeting public, observable requirements over time.

This lowers the barrier to entry and rewards builders who show up — not just the ones who finish first.

---

## 2. Public by Default

Progress must be visible.

- Repositories are public
- Weekly updates are public
- Final presentations are public
- Feedback sessions are recorded

Public participation creates a shared record. It protects participants, protects organizers, and makes the community's work visible to the world.

---

## 3. Verifiable Over Subjective

Every qualification requirement is designed to be independently verifiable.

A weekly tweet either exists or it doesn't.  
A deployed product either works or it doesn't.  
A recorded feedback session either happened or it didn't.

This removes ambiguity and makes the process fair.

---

## 4. Consistency Over Heroics

A builder who shows up every week beats one who disappears for ten weeks and ships a polished product at the end.

The format is designed to reward **sustained effort**, not sprint-and-ship.

This mirrors how real products are built.

---

## 5. Equal Splits Over Tiered Rankings

Within each qualifying track, the reward pool is split equally among all qualified participants.

There is no first place.

This encourages builders to focus on qualifying, not on beating each other.

---

## 6. Low Friction Structure

The rules exist to create clarity, not to create bureaucracy.

Each rule has a clear reason:

- public repositories → visible progress
- weekly tweets → consistent cadence
- registered team members → accountability
- final presentations → verification

Rules that cannot be explained in one sentence should be reconsidered.

---

## 7. Operational Wisdom as Infrastructure

This playbook is not software.

It is **packaged operational wisdom** — lessons from running real events, translated into reusable infrastructure.

The goal is to make it easy for any community to run a high-quality builder event without reinventing every decision from scratch.

---

## 8. Evolve Through Use

The best version of this playbook will come from real use.

Every event that runs using this template should feed lessons back in:

- What worked?
- What needed changing?
- What rules caused confusion?
- What did participants ask about most?

That feedback loop is what turns a one-time format into a durable product.
