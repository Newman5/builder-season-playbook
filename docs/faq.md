# FAQ

## What kind of project can qualify?

A qualifying Builder project must be a user-facing software product with a clear payment gate.

Examples may include:

- SaaS products
- web applications
- web3 applications
- tools with clear user value

Projects that only run locally do not qualify.

## Can I work alone?

Yes. Solo and team projects are both allowed.

## Can I join more than one project?

No. Each participant may only join one project.

## Does every team member need to be named?

Yes. Every contributor must be explicitly listed during registration.

## Do I have to wait until the registration deadline to start building?

No. Once you register, you can start building. What matters is that you have {{event_duration_weeks}} consecutive weeks of building in the repository and updates on {{weekly_update_platform}}.

## Can we use a private repository?

No. The official repository must be public and remain public until final payouts are complete.

## Does the repository need to start empty?

Yes. The official repository must start empty at the beginning of the event.

## What happens if I miss a weekly update?

Missing a required weekly update is disqualifying for Builder qualification.

## What must a weekly update include?

Each required weekly update must:

- clearly show project progress
- include the required hashtags: {{weekly_update_hashtags}}
- mention {{weekly_update_mention}}

## Should I collect evidence of my updates?

Yes. It is up to the builder to collect evidence for the final presentation showing {{event_duration_weeks}} consecutive weekly updates following the required format. The admin team will verify the updates before payout at the end of the event.

## Do I need a deployed product?

For Builder qualification, yes. By the end of the event, the product must be publicly accessible on its intended platform. For qualifying for the Feedback Pie, you don't need to build and deploy a product — only volunteer and be accepted in the Discord channel.

## What counts for the Real User Pie?

You must first qualify through the Builder Pie or {{sponsored_track_name}} requirements, then show at least one real paying user, payment evidence, and a public customer acquisition story.

## What is the {{sponsored_track_name}}?

The {{sponsored_track_name}} is a {{sponsored_track_prize_pool}} {{reward_currency}} reward pool sponsored by {{sponsored_track_sponsor}} for qualifying projects that build on the sponsor's ecosystem.

## Can a {{sponsored_track_name}} project also receive the Builder Pie?

No. For qualifying {{sponsored_track_name}} projects, the {{sponsored_track_name}} replaces the Builder Pie.

## What counts as payment evidence?

Any verifiable evidence that clearly shows a real customer paid for your product can count.

Examples include:

- payment receipts
- paid invoices
- billing records
- bank transfer records
- app store or platform payout records
- blockchain transaction records with enough supporting context

## Can family and friends count as paying users?

No. Family and friends do not count as paying users.

## Do I need to be a Builder to participate in the Feedback Pie?

No. You may participate in the Feedback track even without a builder project.

## Do I get Feedback Pie credits for receiving feedback on my project?

No. Receiving feedback does not earn a Feedback Pie credit by itself. Feedback Pie credits go to the feedback giver who completes a qualifying live, recorded session through the official Discord flow.

## Do builders choose who gives feedback?

Yes. A builder posts the request, feedback givers volunteer in the thread, and the builder team may accept or refuse a volunteer before the recorded session starts.

## How do feedback sessions start?

Join the {{community_name}} Discord, complete server verification, and use the official feedback forum.

Flow:

- a builder posts a feedback request
- a feedback giver volunteers in the thread
- the builder team may accept or refuse the volunteer before the recorded session starts
- the session must be live and recorded to count

- Discussion channel: {{discord_discussion_channel_url}}

## What is the recommended tool to record a feedback session?

{{feedback_platform_recommendation}} is recommended because the free account allows for recorded sessions. Any comparable service is also acceptable if it creates a shareable recording that can be verified.

## How can I raise questions or issues about the event?

If a rule, edge case, or interpretation is unclear, open an issue in this repository.

When opening an issue:

- use a clear title
- describe the question in plain language
- link to the relevant doc section if possible
- include a concrete example or scenario
- keep one issue focused on one question

Admins may use those issues to publish clarifications or record formal rulings later.
